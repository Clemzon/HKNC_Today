What is Creative Corner?

Creative Corner is a community space for participants who want to share their talents, ideas, and projects with their peers. It is designed for students interested in content creation, writing, digital media, technology, social media, design, art, and other creative work.

Whether someone is building a YouTube channel, writing articles, creating digital art, producing music, exploring sports writing, or learning how to create online content, this page can help give their work a place to be seen and appreciated.

How to Share Your Work

  1. Create something you would like others to see.
  2. Submit your work, file, or link using the approved form.
  3. Your submission will be reviewed before being posted.
  4. Once approved, it can be shared here for the campus community.

Submissions may include links, written content, descriptions, artwork, media projects, or other creative work depending on how the page is managed.

Examples of What You Can Share

  • A YouTube channel or video series
  • A written article or blog-style post
  • A sports recap or commentary piece
  • Digital artwork or visual design
  • A music or spoken-word recording
  • An app idea or technology concept
  • A website or coding project
  • A creative portfolio or personal showcase

Why Creative Corner Matters

Creative Corner gives participants a place to be seen, supported, and encouraged by their peers. It helps build confidence, highlights talent, and creates room for students to explore goals connected to writing, media, design, technology, and self-expression.

It can also serve as a stepping stone for students who are curious about careers in content creation, journalism, social media, digital design, app development, or other creative and technical fields.

Guidelines

  • Keep submissions respectful and school-appropriate.
  • Do not include highly personal or private information.
  • Only submit work you created or have permission to share.
  • Submissions may be reviewed before being posted.
  • Content may be removed if it no longer fits the page guidelines.
  • Accessibility and participant safety should remain priorities.
